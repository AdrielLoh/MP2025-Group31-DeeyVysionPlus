How to Protect Yourself Against Deepfakes

Deepfakes pose significant risks, but there are strategies you can use to protect yourself. Below are key measures you can take to mitigate these risks, along with information on the relevant Singaporean government bodies you can contact if you become a victim of deepfake technology.

Stay Informed and Aware

Being aware of the latest trends in deepfake technology is your first line of defense. Here’s how you can stay informed:


Follow Reputable Sources: Regularly check technology news websites, cybersecurity blogs, and official announcements from government bodies for updates on deepfake technology.

Participate in Workshops and Seminars: Engage in workshops, seminars, and online courses that focus on digital literacy, media manipulation, and cybersecurity.

Use Trusted Platforms: Be cautious of unfamiliar websites or social media platforms that could spread misinformation or deepfake content. Stick to well-known, reputable sources for news and information.

Enhance Online Privacy

Protecting your online privacy can significantly reduce your risk of becoming a target of deepfakes. Here are some key steps:


Limit Personal Information: Be mindful of the personal information and images you share on social media. The more information available about you online, the easier it is for someone to create a deepfake.

Adjust Privacy Settings: Use the privacy settings on social media platforms to control who can view your content. Consider making your profiles private to limit access to your personal information.

Be Wary of Phishing Attempts: Be cautious of suspicious emails, messages, or links that could be phishing attempts designed to steal your personal information.

Legal Protection

Understanding your legal rights and the steps you can take if you are a victim of deepfake technology is crucial. Here’s what you can do:


Know Your Rights: Familiarize yourself with the laws surrounding privacy, defamation, and cybercrime in your country. In Singapore, deepfake technology used for malicious purposes may fall under laws like the Protection from Harassment Act (POHA) or the Computer Misuse Act.

Consult a Lawyer: If you believe you have been a victim of a deepfake, seek legal advice to understand your options for recourse. A lawyer can help you navigate the legal system and take appropriate action.


If you encounter a malicious deepfake, report it to the relevant authorities. In Singapore, you can contact the following government bodies:
Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA): CSA provides resources and support for cyber threats.
More information is availiable at: www.csa.gov.sg.

Infocomm Media Development Authority (IMDA): IMDA regulates media content and can be contacted if you find harmful deepfake content online.
More information is available at: www.imda.gov.sg.

Singapore Police Force (SPF): For criminal activities involving deepfakes, you can file a report with the SPF.
More information is availiable at: www.police.gov.sg.

Use Digital Tools

Leveraging digital tools and technologies can help you protect yourself from deepfakes. Here are some recommended actions:


Install Anti-Deepfake Software: Consider using software designed to detect deepfakes. Tools like Deepware or Sensity can analyze content and alert you if it’s likely to be fake.

Monitor Online Presence: Regularly search for your name and images online to see if any unauthorized deepfake content is being circulated.

Enable Content Alerts: Set up Google Alerts or similar services to notify you when new content related to your name or likeness appears online.
