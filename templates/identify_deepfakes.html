How to Identify Deepfakes

Deepfakes are becoming increasingly sophisticated, making them difficult to detect with the naked eye. However, by understanding common signs and using the right tools, you can protect yourself from being fooled. Below are some key areas to focus on when trying to identify deepfakes.

Visual Clues

Deepfakes often have subtle visual inconsistencies that can give them away. Here are some specific things to look out for:

Lighting and Shadows

Check if the lighting on the face is consistent with the background or other parts of the video. Mismatched lighting or unnatural shadows can indicate manipulation.

Facial Movements

Pay attention to eye movements and blinks. Deepfakes may have unnatural eye movements, or the eyes may not blink at all or blink too frequently.

Lip Sync Issues

In many deepfakes, the synchronization between speech and lip movement is not perfect. If the lips don't match the words being spoken, it's a red flag.

Background Artifacts

Sometimes, the background may show signs of distortion, blurring, or strange artifacts around the edges of the person, especially during fast movements.

Audio Clues

Audio is another area where deepfakes can slip up. Even if the visuals are convincing, the audio might not be. Consider the following:

Speech Patterns

Background Noise

Real videos have consistent background noise. If the background sounds abruptly change, sound distorted, or don't match the setting, the video might be altered.

Sometimes, the voice in a deepfake doesn't quite match the person speaking. The pitch or tone might be slightly off, or the voice might sound too monotone.
