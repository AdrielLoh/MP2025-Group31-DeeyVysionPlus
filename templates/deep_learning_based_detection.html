How the Technology Works

Face Detection

Each video frame is scanned to locate faces using a deep neural net-based face detector. Faces are cropped and prepared for model input.

Deep Learning Analysis

Detected faces are passed into a trained convolutional neural network (CNN) which learns to recognize deepfake artifacts and inconsistencies.

Frame-Wise Prediction

Each frame gets a confidence score and label (real or fake). The results are aggregated to make a final prediction for the video.

Key Advantages

🧠

Learns Complex Patterns

Deep neural networks can model subtle, high-dimensional artifacts that escape human detection.

🔎

Frame-Level Analysis

Each frame is individually assessed, making it robust against partial or localized manipulations.

🔄

Adaptable to New Attacks

With retraining, models can adapt to emerging deepfake techniques and new types of visual forgeries.

Technical Challenges

🎬

Sensitive to Data Quality

Blurry, small, or poorly-lit faces can reduce detection accuracy and reliability.

🌍

Generalization Limitations

Performance may drop on faces from new domains (lighting, ethnicity, occlusion) not seen during training.

⚡️

Computational Demands

Real-time video analysis is resource-intensive, especially on high-resolution or multiple face streams.
